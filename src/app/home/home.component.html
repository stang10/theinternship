The Internship follows Billy McMahon (Vince Vaughn) and Nick Campbell (Owen Wilson), middle aged men feeling outdated in the world around them. After being laid off, they seek out work in other fields. One unconventional technical interview and a little help from some sympathetic judges later, these men embark on their journey as "Nooglers" otherwise known as Google Interns.

They spend the summer competing in teams against other interns in a variety of tasks in order to win guaranteed jobs with Google. Billy and Nick are teamed with other interns seen as rejects: Stuart (Dylan O’Brien), who is incredibly sarcastic and attached to his smartphone; Yo-Yo (Tobit Raphael), who was home-schooled by his controlling mother; and Neha (Tira Sircar), an enthusiastic but quirky lover of nerd culture. The team leader Lyle (Josh Brener) constantly tries to act hip to hide his insecurities. Another intern, Ivy-League graduate Graham (Max Minghella), bullies Billy and Nick's team. Roger Chetty (Aasif Mandvi), the head of the internship program, also expresses his doubts about the older men's abilities. Stuart, Yo-Yo, and Neha see Billy and Nick as useless during a task focused on debugging and send them on a wild-goose chase for the fictional Charles Xavier at Stanford University. But later, during a game of quidditch against Graham's team, Billy rallies his team to a comeback that unifies them as a team, despite ultimately losing after Graham cheats.

When the teams are tasked with developing an app, Billy and Nick convince the team to indulge in a wild night out at a strip club. Each of the characters grow closer together and reach far outside of their comfort zones. At the end of the night, Lyle's drunken antics inspire the team to create an app that guards against reckless phone usage while drunk. They win the task by earning the most downloads. The movie then follows as Nick and Billy stumble onto a love of the programming material they learn throughout the summer, risk their team’s chance at jobs, and ultimately earn spots as members of the Google Workforce. Nick even manages to land a date with Dana (Rose Byrne), a high level Google employee and the woman of his dreams.
